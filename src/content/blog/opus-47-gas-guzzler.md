Opus 4.7 dropped and I did what any reasonable person would do — immediately ran it on everything. Burned through months of credits in a couple of days.

## The Numbers

In my testing, Opus 4.7 High consumes roughly **1.2x to 1.5x** more than base Opus 4.6. That's not a small bump. That's "check your billing page and feel something" territory.

The output is better. The reasoning is sharper. But the cost per session adds up fast when you're using it for real work and not toy prompts.

## The Tier Thing

What I really don't get is the medium / high / low / xhigh tier system. Wasn't the whole point of having Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus that you pick the model that fits your use case? Now I'm picking a model AND a power level? It feels like ordering coffee — just give me the thing, I don't need to configure it.

Pick a model. Pick a tier within the model. Hope you picked the right combination. Get billed differently for each. Cool system, very intuitive.

## Still Worth It?

Honestly? For complex design and architecture work, yeah. Opus 4.7 catches things that 4.6 misses. But for everyday tasks I'm sticking with 4.6. No reason to burn premium fuel on a grocery run.
